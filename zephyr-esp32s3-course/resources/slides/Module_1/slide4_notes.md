# Slide 4 Notes

## Why You Should Learn Zephyr?



* Industry demand for engineers who understand modern RTOS design.
* Works across many chips and vendors, so your skills transfer easily.
* Excellent documentation, CI-tested code, and long-term stability.
* Powerful abstraction layers reduce vendor lock-in.
* Ideal for commercial IoT products that require security + maintainability.

## Key idea

Zephyr gives you portable, in-demand skills for modern IoT products

## Narration

There’s a growing demand for engineers who understand modern RTOS concepts. 
More and more products depend on real-time behavior, and companies want people who can build reliable, multitasking embedded systems.

Zephyr also works across a huge range of chips and vendors. 
So when you learn it once, your skills carry over to many different hardware platforms without starting from scratch each time.

It has excellent documentation, continuous integration testing, and strong long-term support. 
That means you’re working with a framework that’s stable, well-maintained, and built for production.

Zephyr’s abstraction layers help reduce vendor lock-in. 
You can switch microcontrollers or add new hardware while keeping most of your application code the same.

And finally, Zephyr is a great fit for serious IoT products where security, reliability, and maintainability really matter. 
It’s designed for real deployments, not just prototypes. Zephyr is backed by a large consortium of over 45+ member companies, including industry giants like Intel, Nordic Semiconductor, NXP, Analog Devices, and STMicroelectronics. These companies are actively investing in Zephyr, creating a large, stable ecosystem and ensuring its support across a vast array of hardware. For instance, Nordic Semiconductor has based its entire SDK on Zephyr, integrating it directly into its core product offering. Zephyr's contributor base has grown significantly, increasing five-fold since 2017, reaching hundreds of unique monthly contributors. This growth signals a major industry shift, where more developers and engineering teams are not just using Zephyr but actively contributing to its stability and feature set.

The demand for open-source technology talent is generally high, with one report noting that 93% of hiring managers reported difficulty finding sufficient open-source talent. The overall market for Embedded Real-Time Operating Systems (RTOS) for IoT is projected to experience substantial growth, reaching around USD 5.6 billion by 2033 with a 12.2% Compound Annual Growth Rate. This market expansion directly drives demand for skilled RTOS engineers.
